# About Page Audit — shreegauli.com/about

---

## Page Purpose

The About page exists to answer: "Who is this person, and why should I trust them with my marketing budget?" It should give visitors a reason to believe — professional background, working philosophy, maybe a bit of personality. It is the credibility page.

---

## First Impression

I expected new content. What I got was the same `AboutPreview` component that appears on the homepage, wrapped in the same page shell with the same shared sections (TrustBar, ResultsStrip, TestimonialsSection, FinalCTASection).

This is not an About page. This is the homepage with a different URL.

The route exists. The metadata exists ("About Shree Krishna Gauli | Digital Marketing Specialist"). But the content is recycled. If I am a prospect who clicked "Learn More" from the homepage About section, I just saw the same text I already read. That is a trust-breaker.

---

## What Works

The metadata is fine. Title and description are specific, include the name and location. The page loads fast because it is mostly reusing shared components.

The design is consistent with the rest of the site. Same dark theme, same typography, same animation patterns. There is visual continuity, which is good.

---

## What Doesn't Work

**Recycled content.** The entire substantive content of this page is the AboutPreview component — the same 2–3 paragraphs that appear on the homepage. There is no additional story, no background detail, no methodology section, no tools list, no certifications section.

**Bloated with shared sections.** After the brief About text, the page loads TrustBar, ResultsStrip, Testimonials, and FinalCTA. These are all sections the visitor has already seen on the homepage. On a dedicated About page, they feel like padding.

**No photo.** A personal brand with no face. This is a significant trust gap for a freelance consultant. People hire people — and they want to see who they are considering.

**No story.** Why did Shree get into marketing? What is their approach to client work? What tools do they prefer? What kind of companies do they work best with? None of this is addressed.

**No certifications section.** The TrustBar mentions Google and HubSpot certifications but does not show badges, links, or dates. An About page is exactly where these should be prominently displayed.

---

## Content Gaps

- Professional background and career narrative
- Work philosophy or methodology
- Professional photo
- Detailed certifications with badges and dates
- Tools and technologies used
- Types of clients served (company size, industries, budgets)
- A "What working with me looks like" section
- Any personal interests or values (optional but humanizing)

---

## Messaging Issues

The current copy does not differentiate Shree from any other "Digital Marketing Specialist based in Dallas." There is nothing about approach, specialties, or point of view. The About page should be the most human page on the site, and right now it is the most generic.

---

## SEO Issues

- Title tag is fine: "About Shree Krishna Gauli | Digital Marketing Specialist"
- Thin content — the unique content on this page is maybe 100 words before shared sections begin
- No FAQ schema, no Person schema embedded
- Google may see this as a "thin content" page because the majority is duplicated from the homepage
- No internal links to specific services or case studies within the About text

---

## CTA / Conversion Issues

The only CTAs are the shared FinalCTASection ("Get in Touch" and "Download Resume") and the general navigation. No contextual CTA specific to the About page like "See My Work" or "Book a Strategy Call." The `/resume` link is still broken here.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Write original About page content (500–800 words minimum).** Career background, philosophy, approach to client work, and values. This should be unique text that does not appear anywhere else on the site.

2. **Add a professional photo.** It does not need to be a studio headshot — a clean, well-lit photo works fine. But a personal brand site without a face feels impersonal.

3. **Add a certifications section.** Google Analytics, Google Ads, HubSpot, or whatever Shree holds. Show the badges. Link to the verifiable credentials.

4. **Remove or reduce shared sections.** TrustBar, ResultsStrip, and Testimonials are already on the homepage. The About page does not need all of them. Maybe keep one testimonial, contextually placed.

5. **Add a contextual CTA.** After reading about who Shree is, the natural next step is either "See My Work" or "Book a Call." Guide that transition instead of relying on the generic FinalCTA.

6. **Add Person structured data.** This is the ideal page for it — name, jobTitle, worksFor, url, sameAs, knowsAbout.
