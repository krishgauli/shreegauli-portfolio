| Value | Label | Sublabel |
|-------|-------|----------|

### Section 4: Who This Is For (NEW)

**Eyebrow:** Best Fit  
**Title:** Built for teams with a revenue leak — not a traffic problem

**Left column — "You're a good fit if..."**
- You're spending $3K–$10K+/mo on ads and leads aren't converting
- Your follow-up process is manual, slow, or nonexistent
- You've hired agencies before and the reporting was useless
- You need one person who diagnoses and fixes across SEO, ads, and operations
- You're in healthcare, local services, or growth-stage SaaS

**Right column — "This isn't the right fit if..."**
- You need a social media manager to post daily content
- You need brand identity, logo design, or video production
- You want a 20-person agency team with dedicated account managers
- You're pre-revenue and need general business advice
- You need enterprise programmatic advertising

## 6. The 5 Highest-Impact Copy Edits to Make First

These are the 5 changes that produce the biggest shift in conversion and clarity for the least effort. Implement them all today.

### Edit 1: Rewrite the hero headline + subheadline + micro-line

**File:** `src/components/sections/Hero/HeroText.tsx`

**Current headline:**
> Growth systems for brands that need more qualified demand.

**New headline:**
> You're generating leads. You're losing revenue.

**Current subheadline:**
> SEO, AEO/GEO, Google Ads, social growth, fullstack builds, Next.js, mobile app flows, and automation for founders and lean teams that want a clearer path from traffic to pipeline.

**New subheadline:**
> I build the system between your ad spend and your closed deals — SEO, Google Ads, and automation designed so every lead gets followed up, tracked, and converted. For healthcare practices, service businesses, and growth-stage teams.

**Current micro-line:**
> Recent work across healthcare, SaaS, e-commerce, local services, and product teams shipping web and mobile.

**New micro-line:**
> Based in Dallas. Working with healthcare, local services, and SaaS teams across the US.

**Why this is #1:** The hero is seen by 100% of visitors. The current headline is generic and could describe any marketing agency. The new headline calls out a specific pain and creates urgency. The subheadline shifts from listing services to describing a system with a clear outcome.
