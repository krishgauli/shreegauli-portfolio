# Services Pages Blueprint

The services section is the commercial engine of the site. Five pages total: one hub page and four individual service pages. Each service page needs to rank for its own keyword while also clearly showing what you actually get when you hire Shree. The biggest mistake I see on consultant sites is service pages that are vague — "we help you grow" without explaining *how*. These blueprints push for specificity.

## Services Overview (/services)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| URL | /services |
| Primary Keyword | digital marketing services |
| Secondary Keywords | small business marketing services, marketing consultant services |
| Page Purpose | Central hub linking to all service lines, build understanding of full offering |
| Search Intent | Commercial |
| H1 | Digital Marketing Services |
| SEO Title | Digital Marketing Services \| SEO, Paid Media, Social, Automation — Shree Krishna Gauli |
| Meta Description | Full-service digital marketing for small businesses and startups. SEO audits, Google Ads management, social media strategy, and marketing automation. |
| URL Slug | /services |
| Canonical | https://shreegauli.com/services |

### Content Sections
The hub page should function as a gateway. People land here, understand the full offering at a glance, and click into the service they care about most. Don't try to sell everything on this one page — that's what the individual pages are for.

1. **Hero** — H1, subtitle explaining the service philosophy, primary CTA
2. **Service Cards** — 4 cards linking to individual service pages (SEO, Paid Media, Social Media, Automation)
3. **How It Works** — 3-step process overview (Audit → Strategy → Execution)
4. **Results Preview** — Key metrics from case studies
5. **Industries Served** — Brief list of business types served
6. **CTA** — Book a Strategy Call

### Internal Links
- /services/seo, /services/paid-media, /services/social-media, /services/automation
- /work (case studies)
- /pricing
- /lp/book-a-call, /contact
