# Sub-Services Pages Blueprint

## Why These Pages Exist

The main service pages can't rank for everything. A page about "SEO services" isn't going to rank for "SEO audit service" or "local SEO services" — those are different searches with different intent. Sub-service pages fill that gap. They target specific long-tail commercial keywords and serve as entry points for people looking for something very particular.

None of these pages exist yet. Build them when bandwidth allows, in roughly the priority order below.

## Recommended Sub-Service Pages

### Priority 1 — Build These First (Month 2–3)

These three have the highest commercial intent and search volume. They also naturally connect to existing case studies and blog content, which makes them easier to build and link.

## A Note on Navigation

Don't add sub-service pages to the main nav — they'll clutter it. Instead, link to them from their parent service page in a "More SEO Services" or "Related Services" section at the bottom. They're SEO landing pages, not primary destinations. The people who need them will find them through search, and that's exactly the point.
