# About Page Blueprint

The about page is one of those pages that's easy to neglect because it doesn't feel like it "sells" anything. But it does — it sells trust. When someone is considering hiring a consultant, they want to know who they're dealing with. This page needs to make that crystal clear without turning into a memoir.

## Content Sections

The about page should be tight — under 1,000 words of copy. Nobody wants to read a full autobiography before deciding whether to get in touch. Hit the key points and move on.

### 1. Header
- **H1:** About Shree Krishna Gauli
- **Subtitle:** Digital marketing consultant specializing in growth systems for small businesses and startups.

### 2. Professional Story
- **Purpose:** Build trust through an honest background — not a sales pitch, but an explanation of how you got here and what you actually care about
- **Content:**
  - Where you started and what led to this work
  - What kind of problems you solve
  - Your philosophy on marketing (results-first, no fluff)
  - Years of experience and key skills
- **Tone:** Professional, direct, personal but not casual — imagine explaining your work to someone at a conference, not writing a LinkedIn "influencer" post

### 3. What I Do (Services Brief)
- **Purpose:** Quick overview connecting to service pages
- **Content:** 4 brief service descriptions (SEO, Paid Media, Social Media, Automation)
- **Internal Links:** /services/seo, /services/paid-media, /services/social-media, /services/automation

### 4. Results Snapshot
- **Purpose:** Proof of capability
- **Content:** 3 key metrics from case studies
  - 340% organic traffic growth (SEO case study)
  - 4.8x ROAS (Paid Media case study)
  - 20+ hours saved per week (Automation case study)
- **Internal Links:** /work/seo-growth, /work/paid-media, /work/automation

### 5. Expertise Areas
- **Purpose:** E-E-A-T signals for Google
- **Content:** List of specific expertise areas: technical SEO, Google Ads, n8n automation, marketing analytics, content strategy
- **Format:** Grid or icon cards

### 6. How I Work
- **Purpose:** Set expectations for potential clients
- **Content:** Brief overview of engagement process
- **Internal Links:** /working-together, /pricing

### 7. CTA Section
- **Purpose:** Convert interest into action
- **Content:** "Ready to work together?"
- **Internal Links:** /lp/book-a-call, /contact

## What Would Actually Move the Needle

1. **Add structured Person schema** with all the important fields (jobTitle, knowsAbout, worksFor, sameAs). Google uses this to understand who you are — it's worth the 15 minutes.
2. **Add a visible breadcrumb** (Home > About) for both schema and visual navigation. Small thing, but it helps.
3. **Include a professional photo.** I know this seems obvious, but it's critical for E-E-A-T trust signals. People want to see the person they're hiring.
4. **Add client logos or platform badges** — any kind of social proof beyond words.
5. **Keep the copy concise.** Under 1,000 words. The about page should make someone want to explore the rest of the site, not give them everything upfront.
