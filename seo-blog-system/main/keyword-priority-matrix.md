# Keyword Priority Matrix

## How This Works

Every keyword in the top-100 list has a home — a specific page on the site where it belongs. This matrix connects those keywords to pages and gives each one a clear action: optimize what exists, create something new, or wait until we've built enough authority.

One page, one primary keyword. That's the rule. Supporting pages can mention the same topic, but there should be exactly one page on the site that "owns" each keyword. This avoids the cannibalization problem where two of your own pages compete against each other and both end up underperforming.

The tiers below aren't arbitrary. They're based on a mix of how hard the keyword is to rank for, whether we already have relevant content, and how directly it ties to booking a call or running an audit. Some P1 keywords are P1 simply because the post already exists and just needs a refresh. Others need to be created, but the difficulty is low enough that a solid piece of content can rank relatively quickly.

I'll be honest — the timelines are estimates. If something takes longer than expected, that's fine. The order matters more than the speed.

## Priority Tiers

| Tier | Timeline | What qualifies |
|------|----------|----------------|
| P1 — Immediate | Month 1–2 | Low difficulty, high relevance, content already exists or is easy to create. These are the quick wins. |
| P2 — Short-term | Month 2–4 | Medium difficulty, strong relevance, but requires new content. Some of these are pillar posts that take time to write well. |
| P3 — Mid-term | Month 4–6 | Medium difficulty, decent relevance, but they work best once supporting content is already published. |
| P4 — Long-term | Month 6–12 | Higher difficulty or broader topics. We need more domain authority and internal linking before these have a realistic shot. |

These are the low-hanging fruit, and I don't use that phrase lightly. Most of these keywords already have a published blog post that just needs a refresh — updated title, better meta description, tighter internal linking. The biggest opportunity here is probably the SEO tools page. It already exists, it already gets some traffic, and keywords like "free seo audit tool" and "website seo checker" have real commercial intent. Tightening up the on-page SEO there could produce results within weeks, not months.

## P4 — Long-term (Month 6–12)

These are the stretch goals. Some have genuinely high volume but also genuinely stiff competition. Others are niche enough that we could rank for them, but they only convert if we've already built enough trust through the rest of the content.

I'd revisit this tier every quarter. What feels unreachable now might become feasible once the site has 30–40 published blog posts and stronger internal linking. Don't force these early — that's how you end up with mediocre content that doesn't rank and doesn't convert.

## Pages Carrying Multiple Keywords

A few pages naturally attract more than one keyword. That's fine — service pages especially should optimize for a primary keyword while picking up related queries. Just make sure the page isn't trying to be everything to everyone. A focused page that serves one intent well will outrank a diluted page that tries to cover five topics.
