# Internal Linking Plan

## Why This Matters More Than People Think

Internal linking is probably the most underrated SEO lever on any site. It's free, it's entirely within your control, and it directly affects how Google discovers and values your pages. On this site, it also serves a practical UX purpose — helping readers find related content and guiding them toward a CTA.

Three jobs, really:
1. **Pass authority** — the homepage has the most link equity, and internal links distribute it to the pages that need it
2. **Help people navigate** — if someone finishes reading about SEO audits and you don't link to the audit tool, that's a missed opportunity
3. **Guide toward conversion** — every content page should have a natural path to a service page, a tool, or a booking link

---

## Link Flow Hierarchy

```
Homepage (highest authority)
  ↓
Service Pages + Blog Hub + Case Studies Hub
  ↓
Individual Blog Posts + Individual Case Studies
  ↓
CTAs: /contact, /lp/book-a-call, /seo-tools, /pricing
```

The homepage distributes authority downward. Blog posts and case studies link laterally to each other and upward to service pages.

## Anchor Text — The Words You Click On

This might seem trivial, but the words you use as anchor text send signals to Google about what the linked page is about. "Click here" tells Google nothing. "Our technical SEO audit checklist" tells it exactly what's on the other end.

### What to do
- Use descriptive, keyword-relevant anchor text
- Vary anchor text for the same target page across different posts
- Match the anchor text to what the target page is about
- Use natural language that fits the sentence

### What not to do
- Use "click here", "read more", "learn more" as anchor text
- Use the exact same anchor text for the same page across every post
- Stuff exact-match keywords as anchor text unnaturally
- Link the same target page more than twice in one post
- Use naked URLs as anchor text

### Good examples vs. bad examples

I know this is obvious to some people, but I've seen it done wrong enough times that it's worth spelling out:

**Good:**
- "Check out our [technical SEO audit checklist](/blogs/technical-seo-audit-checklist-service-businesses) for a detailed walkthrough."
- "We helped one SaaS client achieve [340% organic traffic growth](/work/seo-growth) in 12 months."
- "If you're comparing options, read [freelancer vs. agency for marketing](/blogs/freelancer-vs-agency-marketing)."

**Bad:**
- "[Click here](/blogs/technical-seo-audit-checklist-service-businesses) to read our checklist."
- "Read about our results [here](/work/seo-growth)."
- "[SEO services SEO consultant SEO audit](/services/seo)."

## What Order to Tackle This

Don't try to do all of this at once. Work in layers:

1. **Done:** All `/blog/` links fixed to `/blogs/` (completed in the code changes)
2. **Week 1:** Add blog-to-service links in the existing 10 posts — this is the highest-leverage change
3. **Week 2:** Add service-to-blog links on the service pages
4. **Week 3:** Add blog-to-blog cross-links in existing posts
5. **Ongoing:** Every new blog post must include all required internal links before publishing. Make it part of the quality checklist, not an afterthought.
