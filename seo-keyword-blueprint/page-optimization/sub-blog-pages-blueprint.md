# Sub-Blog Pages Blueprint

## Purpose

Sub-blog pages are supporting cluster articles. They exist to deepen topical authority, capture adjacent intent, and strengthen internal linking around pillar pages.

## Best Supporting Articles

| Supporting post | Cluster it supports | Main destination |
|---|---|---|
| What to fix first after an SEO audit | SEO tool / audit | `/services/seo-audit` |
| Keyword research mistakes that waste content budget | SEO strategy | `/services/keyword-research` |
| Local SEO signals that matter for service businesses | Local SEO | `/services/local-seo` |

## Content Pattern

- Shorter than the main pillar article
- Built around one sub-question or objection
- Designed to link up to a main service or pillar post
- Can be more tactical and example-driven

## Internal Linking Rules

- Link to the pillar post in the intro or first third
- Link to one relevant service page
- Link to one case study if proof is relevant
- Receive links back from the hub and pillar article

## Trust Elements

- Mini examples
- Short screenshots
- One quoted lesson from real client work when possible

## Conversion Elements

- Soft CTA mid-article
- Hard CTA at the end
- Tool CTA only on SEO-relevant posts

## SEO Notes

- Avoid making supporting posts compete with the pillar term
- Use modifiers like `checklist`, `mistakes`, `what to fix first`, `examples`, `process`, or `vs`

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Publishing supporting posts before the pillar or service page exists
- Letting support posts become duplicate versions of the main article
