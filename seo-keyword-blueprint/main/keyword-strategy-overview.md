# Keyword Strategy Overview

## Business Snapshot

Shreegauli.com is positioned as a consultant-led growth site, not a broad agency brand. The strongest current offer is a mix of:

- SEO and content strategy
- Google Ads and paid media management
- Social media growth systems
- Marketing automation, n8n workflows, CRM integration, and reporting
- A real free SEO audit tool that can attract top-of-funnel traffic

The likely buyers are founders, owner-operators, and lean marketing leads who need one senior operator who can diagnose problems, execute, and show measurable outcomes without agency overhead.

## Core SEO Reality

- No Google Search Console export or query dataset was available in the repo, so this plan does not pretend to use first-party query data.
- Search demand figures below are best-effort U.S. monthly estimates built from publicly exposed Semrush keyword data plus manual inference for close variants.
- Trend direction was checked with Google Trends U.S. 5-year snapshots and supported with current market research where relevant.
- The site already deserves to compete for consultant-led service terms. It does not yet deserve to chase broad agency vanity terms at scale.

## Main Buyer Problems

- They have traffic or revenue bottlenecks but unclear diagnosis.
- They need channel strategy tied to outcomes, not generic reporting.
- They want direct senior execution, not an account-manager layer.
- They need systems that compound: SEO assets, better ad economics, cleaner attribution, and automation that saves time.

## Best-Fit Keyword Themes

| Theme | Why It Fits | Commercial Value | SEO Reality |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Digital marketing consultant | Matches the personal-brand positioning better than "agency" | Very high | Competitive but winnable with strong service architecture |
| SEO consultant / SEO services | Strongest service-market fit and best content flywheel | Very high | Must be supported by service depth, case studies, and blog clusters |
| SEO audit / SEO tool terms | Strong differentiator because the site has a real tool | High | Best short-term non-brand acquisition opportunity |
| Google Ads / PPC consultant | High-intent buyer terms tied to paid media page | Very high | Best handled through one paid media pillar plus sub-service pages |
| Marketing automation / n8n consultant | Clear niche angle and lower-competition commercial intent | High | Rising interest; good place to win qualified demand earlier |
| Social media consultant | Relevant, but weaker SEO moat than SEO or automation | Medium | Target carefully; avoid publishing filler social content |
| Comparison / evaluation queries | Pulls in warm buyers evaluating options | High | Best served by blog and proof pages, not the homepage |
| Case-study / proof queries | Low volume but strong conversion assistance | High | Critical for trust and internal linking, even if traffic is modest |

## Strategic Decisions

1. The homepage should target `digital marketing consultant`, not try to rank for every service phrase equally.
2. `/services/seo`, `/services/paid-media`, and `/services/automation` should carry the highest commercial SEO burden.
3. `/seo-tools` should become a search acquisition engine around `free seo audit`, `seo audit tool`, and `website seo checker`.
4. Social media terms should stay secondary unless more social-specific proof is added.
5. Case studies should target proof-intent terms and feed conversion, not just exist as portfolio pieces.
6. Do not create city-page spam. One Dallas angle is enough unless local proof depth materially improves.

## 90-Day SEO Build Order

### Phase 1: Fastest Commercial Wins

- Reposition homepage around `digital marketing consultant`
- Upgrade `/services/seo` around `seo consultant`
- Upgrade `/services/paid-media` around `google ads consultant`
- Upgrade `/services/automation` around `marketing automation consultant`
- Rework `/seo-tools` around `free seo audit` and `seo audit tool`

### Phase 2: Supportive Intent and Trust

- Publish 3 deeper case studies targeting SEO, paid media, and automation proof terms
- Publish 6 blog posts tied to consultant-evaluation and service-comparison intent
- Add stronger internal links between tool, service, blog, and case-study pages

### Phase 3: Expansion

- Add sub-service pages only where intent is real: SEO audit, local SEO, Google Ads audit, n8n automation, CRM automation, reporting dashboards
- Add one pricing / working-together page
- Add one FAQ / process page if sales calls repeat the same objections

## What Not To Do

- Do not build broad agency-style content around terms like `digital marketing agency` unless the business wants agency positioning.
- Do not spin out thin service pages for every platform.
- Do not publish generic top-of-funnel posts like `what is seo` before comparison, audit, and buyer-intent content is live.
- Do not create separate pages for every city.
